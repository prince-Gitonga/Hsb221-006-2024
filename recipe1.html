Ingredients

  • 200g spaghetti
  • 100g guanciale (or pancetta), cubed
  • 2 large eggs
  • 50g Pecorino Romano cheese, grated
  • Black pepper, freshly ground
  • Salt

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook according to package directions until al dente.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, gently fry the guanciale in a pan over medium heat until crispy. Remove from the heat and set aside, reserving some of the rendered fat.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated Pecorino Romano cheese, and a generous amount of freshly ground black pepper. Add a tablespoon or two of the reserved guanciale fat and whisk again.
  4. Drain the spaghetti, reserving about a cup of the pasta water.
  5. Add the hot spaghetti to the pan with the guanciale. Toss to combine.
  6. Remove the pan from the heat. Pour the egg and cheese mixture over the hot pasta, tossing vigorously to create a creamy sauce. If the sauce seems too thick, add a little of the reserved pasta water until it reaches the desired consistency.
  7. Serve immediately, garnished with extra grated Pecorino Romano cheese and black pepper.

Optional Nutritional Information (per serving)

Calories: ~400kcal | Protein: ~18g | Fat: ~25g | Carbohydrates: ~30g
